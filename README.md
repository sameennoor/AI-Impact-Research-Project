# AI: Friend or Foe?
A research-based academic project exploring public perceptions of Artificial Intelligence (AI) and whether it is viewed as a helpful companion or a potential threat. The study analyzes opinions regarding AI’s impact on daily life, jobs, privacy, education, creativity, and human decision-making.
The research was conducted through a structured survey containing 18 questions and responses collected from students, teachers, and IT professionals.
# 📊  Core Areas of Research
1. ## AI in Daily Life
Exploring how AI improves convenience, automation, productivity, and everyday digital experiences while also increasing dependency on intelligent systems.

2. ## Employment & Future Workforce
Analyzing concerns regarding job displacement, automation-driven workforce changes, and the need for continuous skill adaptation in the AI era.

3. ## Privacy & Trust in AI Systems
Investigating public hesitation toward sharing sensitive information with AI-powered platforms and concerns surrounding data misuse and surveillance.

4. ## AI in Education & Creativity
Evaluating both the positive and negative impacts of AI in learning environments, including creativity enhancement, academic assistance, and misuse in educational settings.

5. ## Ethics, Regulation & Human Control
Studying public opinion regarding AI governance, ethical boundaries, regulatory frameworks, and the importance of maintaining human involvement in critical decisions.

# 🔬 Research Methodology
1. ## Survey Design
A structured 18-question survey was designed to collect opinions related to AI usage, trust, ethics, safety, and societal impact.

2. ## Participant Demographics
Responses were collected from students, teachers, and IT professionals representing diverse educational and professional backgrounds.

3. ## Data Analysis
Survey responses were analyzed using percentage-based evaluations and pie chart visualizations to identify common trends, concerns, and public sentiment patterns.

# Major Findings
- Most participants actively use AI technologies in daily life
- AI is largely viewed as a tool that saves time and increases efficiency
- Privacy and data security remain major concerns
- Many respondents prefer human supervision in AI-driven decisions
- Concerns about job replacement and overdependence on AI continue to exist
- Overall public perception leans toward AI being a supportive and beneficial technology rather than a direct threat
  # Conclusion
The study concludes that while people recognize the risks associated with Artificial Intelligence, they largely view AI as a beneficial technology that can improve efficiency, creativity, and productivity when used responsibly and ethically.
